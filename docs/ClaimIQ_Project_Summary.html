Executive Summary
Platform overview, core capabilities, and stakeholder value

ClaimIQ is a full-stack Medicare Part B drug spend intelligence platform built to demonstrate how modern data engineering, machine learning, and AI-powered natural language interfaces can transform raw government claims data into decision-grade financial intelligence — the kind of analysis that currently takes healthcare analytics teams days to produce manually.

1.1 Platform Overview

The platform ingests the publicly available CMS Medicare Part B Quarterly Drug Spending dataset — covering 1,759 drug billing records across 2024 (full year, Q1–Q4) and 2025 (partial year, Q1–Q3) — and applies a three-layer analytical stack on top of it:

  • Machine Learning Layer: Two models run at startup — a Prophet time series model that projects full-year 2025 spending for the top 20 drugs, and an Isolation Forest anomaly detector that scores all drugs on financial irregularity.
  • Statistical Layer: A linear regression variance model identifies drugs where 2025 actual spending deviates significantly from what 2024 data would predict — surfacing unexpected cost behaviour beyond simple year-over-year comparison.
  • AI Conversational Layer: A Groq-powered LLaMA 3.3 70B assistant with full access to the dataset, model outputs, anomaly scores, and forecasts — capable of answering specific CFO-level questions about any drug in the portfolio and generating structured executive briefs on demand.

Project Background
Medicare Part B context, problem statement, and design rationale

2.1 Medicare Part B and the Drug Spend Problem

Medicare Part B covers physician-administered drugs — medications infused or injected in a physician's office, hospital outpatient department, or clinic setting. Unlike Part D (pharmacy-dispensed drugs), Part B drugs are billed directly by the provider and reimbursed by CMS at Average Sales Price (ASP) plus 6%. This reimbursement model creates a distinct financial profile: a relatively small number of high-cost biologics and specialty drugs account for an outsized share of total program spend.

As of 2024, Medicare Part B drug spending represents one of the fastest-growing components of Medicare expenditure. CMS publishes quarterly drug spending data to enable transparency and oversight — but the raw CSV files require significant analytical work before they yield actionable intelligence. Health plan analysts and pharmacy directors currently spend hours each quarter manually building spreadsheets to answer questions that a well-designed platform can answer in seconds.

2.3 Design Philosophy

ClaimIQ was designed around three principles borrowed from professional healthcare analytics practice:

  1. Data before AI: All machine learning models run on startup against clean, validated data. The AI layer consumes model outputs — it does not replace them. Forecasts, anomaly scores, and variance flags are computed deterministically before any language model sees the data.
  2. Transparency over black boxes: Every metric shown in the dashboard has a documented calculation methodology. Discount rates cite MedPAC; anomaly scores cite the Isolation Forest contamination parameter; forecasts show confidence intervals alongside projections.
  3. Operator-first design: The dashboard is built for a health plan CFO, not a data scientist. Every chart has a plain-English interpretation. The AI assistant refuses off-topic questions and grounds every answer in specific drug names and dollar figures from the dataset.

5.2 Data Pipeline Transformation Flow

The following steps are executed sequentially inside data_pipeline.py every time the backend starts:

  1. Load CSV: Read the 1,759-row CMS file from backend/data/ using a relative path.
  2. Strip whitespace: Normalise all string columns to prevent merge failures from trailing spaces.
  3. Create drug_key: Cast HCPCS_Cd to string — used as the unique identifier throughout the platform.
  4. Year split: Separate into df_2024 (~880 rows) and df_2025 (~879 rows) on the Year column.
  5. Inner merge: Join on drug_key to produce df_merged — only drugs present in both years, with _2024 and _2025 column suffixes.
  6. Derived metrics: Compute spend_change_abs, spend_change_pct, cost_per_claim_change_pct, cost_per_bene_change_pct.
  7. Variance flags: Apply high_increase / stable / high_decrease at ±20% spend change threshold.
  8. Discount tiers: Assign WAC-to-ASP discount rate by drug tier using Avg_Spnd_Per_Clm_2024 as proxy; compute estimated_market_discount_2024 and estimated_net_asp_cost_2024.

6.1 Prophet — Time Series Forecasting

FORECASTING

Prophet is an open-source time series forecasting library developed by Meta. It is designed for business time series that exhibit non-linear trends with strong seasonality, and it is robust to missing data and outliers.

Why Prophet for this dataset?

The CMS dataset provides only two annual data points per drug — 2024 full year and 2025 Q1–Q3 partial year. Traditional ARIMA or LSTM models require significantly more historical data points. Prophet can fit a meaningful trend line on two points and extrapolate forward, making it the practical choice for this data structure.

How it is applied

  1. Annualisation: 2025 Q1–Q3 spend is multiplied by (12/9) to create a full-year equivalent, providing two comparable annual data points.
  2. Prophet DataFrame: A 2-row dataset is prepared per drug with ds = ['2024-12-31', '2025-09-30'] and y = [actual_2024_spend, actual_2025_spend].
  3. Seasonality disabled: yearly_seasonality=False, weekly_seasonality=False, daily_seasonality=False — because with only two annual points, no seasonality pattern can be estimated.
  4. Forecast horizon: 3 quarterly periods forward from the last data point to project end-of-2025 spend.
  5. Outputs: yhat (point projection), yhat_lower, yhat_upper (80% confidence interval), growth_rate_pct vs 2024.

6.2 Isolation Forest — Anomaly Detection

ANOMALY DETECTION

Isolation Forest is an unsupervised machine learning algorithm that identifies anomalies by measuring how easily a data point can be isolated from the rest of the population. Anomalous points — those that are unusual in their combination of features — are isolated with fewer random splits and receive a lower decision function score.

Why Isolation Forest for drug spend?

Drug spend data is highly skewed — a small number of specialty biologics dominate spend while thousands of lower-cost drugs form a long tail. Standard statistical methods (e.g. standard deviation thresholds) fail in this distribution. Isolation Forest is specifically designed for high-dimensional, skewed datasets and does not assume a normal distribution.

How it is applied

  1. Feature set (5 variables): Tot_Spndng, Tot_Clms, Tot_Benes, Avg_Spnd_Per_Clm, Avg_Spnd_Per_Bene — all from the 2024 dataset.
  2. StandardScaler: All features are z-scored before fitting. This is mandatory because Isolation Forest is scale-sensitive — without scaling, Tot_Spndng (billions) would dominate Avg_Spnd_Per_Clm (hundreds).
  3. Model parameters: n_estimators=100, contamination=0.05 (expect 5% of drugs to be anomalous), random_state=42.
  4. Score inversion: decision_function() returns higher values for normal points. Scores are inverted and normalised to 0–1 so that 1 = most anomalous.
  5. Reason generation: Plain-English anomaly reasons are generated using percentile thresholds: extreme cost per claim (> 99th percentile), unusually high claims per patient (> 95th percentile ratio), high spend in few patients (> 95th spend & < 25th beneficiary count).

6.3 Linear Regression — Variance Analysis

VARIANCE ANALYSIS

The variance analysis module uses ordinary least squares linear regression to establish a baseline expectation for 2025 drug spend based on each drug's 2024 financial profile. Drugs that deviate significantly from this expectation are flagged as "above" or "below" model expectation — a signal that goes beyond simple percentage change.

Why regression-based variance?

Simple year-over-year percentage change has a limitation: a drug that grows 25% but had low 2024 volume is very different from a drug that grows 25% at $500M in spend. Linear regression accounts for the relationship between a drug's 2024 claims volume, cost per claim, and beneficiary count to predict where 2025 spend should land — then surfaces the drugs that deviate most from that prediction.

How it is applied

  1. Features: Tot_Clms_2024, Avg_Spnd_Per_Clm_2024, Tot_Benes_2024.
  2. Target: Tot_Spndng_2025.
  3. Fit: sklearn LinearRegression on the merged dataset.
  4. Residuals: model_residual = actual_2025 − predicted_2025.
  5. Z-scoring: Residuals are standardised (residual_zscore). |z| > 2 is flagged as significant variance — approximately the top/bottom 5% of deviations.
  6. Labels: "Above expectation" (z > 2), "Below expectation" (z < −2), "As expected" otherwise.

6.4 WAC-to-ASP Market Discount Methodology

FINANCIAL METHODOLOGY

The CMS dataset does not contain rebate data. Medicare Part B reimburses providers at Average Sales Price (ASP) + 6% — ASP already reflects market discounts negotiated between manufacturers and all their customers. What the platform estimates is the WAC-to-ASP differential: the gap between a drug's list price (Wholesale Acquisition Cost) and the actual transaction price (ASP) that CMS uses for reimbursement.

Drug Tier Threshold (Cost/Claim) Discount Rate Source
Specialty Biologic > $10,000/claim 25% MedPAC March 2023 Report, Ch.5 — biologics avg 22–28% WAC-to-ASP discount
Brand Drug $1,000 – $10,000/claim 20% MedPAC March 2023 Report — brand drugs avg 18–23% WAC-to-ASP discount
Generic / Low-Cost < $1,000/claim 3% MedPAC March 2023 — generics 2–5%; ASP already competitive

Important: These discounts are already embedded in the ASP-based spending figures shown in the dataset. The "Net ASP Cost" and "WAC-ASP Discount" chart shows the estimated WAC list price decomposition — not an additional rebate on top of what CMS paid.

Results Summary
Portfolio-level findings, model outputs, and recommended actions

7.1 Portfolio-Level Findings

The CMS dataset captures Medicare Part B drug spending across approximately 880 unique drugs appearing in both 2024 and 2025. The portfolio exhibits characteristics typical of specialty-dominated drug spend: a small number of high-cost biologics drive the majority of program dollars, while a long tail of lower-cost drugs accounts for the majority of claim volume.

  • Spend concentration is high: The top 10 drugs by 2024 spend account for a significant majority of total Medicare Part B drug expenditure — a structural portfolio risk if any single drug faces formulary disruption, indication expansion, or pricing change.
  • Year-over-year trend: 2025 Q1–Q3 partial-year spend, when compared to the equivalent 2024 period, reflects ongoing growth in specialty biologic utilisation — consistent with CMS and MedPAC national trend data.
  • Cost per claim divergence: Average cost per claim varies by several orders of magnitude across the portfolio — from under $100 for generic injectables to over $100,000 for rare disease biologics — confirming the need for tier-based financial management.

7.3 Recommended Actions

  1. Prioritise utilisation management review for anomaly-flagged top spenders. Drugs that appear in both the top-20 by spend AND the top-10 anomaly list represent the highest financial risk. These drugs should be the first candidates for PA (prior authorisation) review, step therapy requirements, or preferred product substitution analysis.
  2. Flag high-increase drugs for contract renegotiation or biosimilar analysis. Drugs with >20% YoY spend increase that also exceed model expectation (z > 2) are likely experiencing indication expansion or pricing drift. These are prime candidates for value-based contract discussions or biosimilar transition planning where applicable.
  3. Use forecast confidence bands to size budget reserves. For the top 5 drugs by projected 2025 spend, the width of the Prophet confidence interval (yhat_upper − yhat_lower) quantifies forecast uncertainty. Finance teams should hold reserves proportional to this band rather than using point estimates for budget planning.
  4. Investigate concentration risk. A portfolio where 10 drugs represent the majority of total spend is exposed to significant volatility risk. Any formulary disruption, FDA safety action, or biosimilar entry for a top-3 drug would materially impact program spend. Scenario modelling for these events is recommended.
  5. Validate WAC-to-ASP discount capture against actual contract terms. The estimated market discounts in this platform are based on MedPAC aggregate benchmarks. Actual contracted rates will differ by plan and manufacturer. Finance teams should overlay actual contract discount data to refine the net cost estimates.
